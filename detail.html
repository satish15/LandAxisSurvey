Topographic Surveys

Objective: To map the natural and man-made features of a site.
Details: Measurement of elevations, contours, and terrain variations. Identification of features such as roads, buildings, trees, and utilities. Preparation of detailed topographic maps for project planning. Applications: Infrastructure development, urban planning, and environmental assessments.

2. Boundary Surveys

Objective: To define the legal boundaries of a property.
Details: Identification of existing property lines and encroachments. Establishment of permanent boundary markers. Preparation of boundary survey reports for legal documentation. Applications: Property transactions, land disputes, and zoning compliance.

3.Contour Mapping

Objective: To create detailed contour maps showing elevation changes.
Details: Measurement of ground levels at regular intervals. Generation of contour lines representing elevation differences. Use of digital tools to create 3D terrain models. Applications: Drainage design, road construction, and landscape planning.

4. Construction Layout Surveys

Objective: To guide construction activities by marking reference points.
Details: Setting out the exact locations of structures, roads, and utilities. Verification of design alignment with site conditions. Continuous monitoring during construction to ensure accuracy. Applications: Building construction, infrastructure projects, and industrial facilities.

5. Route Surveys

Objective: To plan and design linear infrastructure projects.
Details: Surveying routes for roads, railways, pipelines, and power lines. Identifying optimal alignments based on terrain and environmental factors. Providing data for detailed route design and construction planning. Applications: Transportation, energy, and utility projects.

6. As-Built Surveys

Objective: To document the current state of a completed or partially completed project.
Details: Verification of construction accuracy against design specifications. Measurement of completed structures, utilities, and site features. Preparation of as-built drawings and records. Applications: Compliance reporting, maintenance planning, and project handovers.

7. Volume Calculation Surveys

Objective: To calculate the volume of earthwork or materials on a site.
Details: Measurement of existing ground levels and site features. Comparison with proposed designs or excavation plans. Accurate computation of cut-and-fill volumes for construction. Applications: Earthworks, mining, and landfill projects.
